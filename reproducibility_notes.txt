Reproducibility Notes: Random Seeds and Data Ordering
=====================================================

This document summarizes the random seed settings used in the experiments and clarifies
the deterministic data-ordering conventions adopted in the real-data scripts.

1. Repeated Synthetic Experiments
---------------------------------

The main repeated synthetic experiments are based on 50 replications.

(a) simulations/sim_setting1_syn.R
- Master seed used in `run_sim_parallel()`: 2025
- Number of replications in the executed experiment: R = 50
- Realized per-replication data-generation seeds: 2026, 2027, ..., 2075
- The data generator `gen_beta_shift_only()` has default seed = 2025
- The parallel loop is executed with `future.seed = TRUE`

(b) simulations/sim_setting2_syn.R
- Master seed used in `run_sim_parallel_sim2()`: 2025
- Number of replications in the executed experiment: R = 50
- Realized per-replication data-generation seeds: 2026, 2027, ..., 2075
- The data generator `gen_wendland()` is called with seed = 2025 + i
- The parallel loop is executed with `future.seed = TRUE`

Accordingly, for the two main repeated synthetic experiments, the primary master seed is 2025,
and the realized dataset-generation seeds are 2026 through 2075.

2. Single-Run Synthetic Illustration Scripts
--------------------------------------------

(a) simulations/sim_local_syn.R
- Setting 1 single-run seed: 2020
- Setting 2 single-run seed: 10
- Note: although the helper generator for Setting 1 has default seed = 2025, the executed
  example in this script uses seed = 2020

(b) simulations/sim_robust.R
- Synthetic data seed used in the executed script: 2026
